The Sunnylands Center & Gardens in Rancho Mirage, California, is a 15-acre extension of the Annenberg estate commissioned in 2006 that has dramatically curtailed water use. To respond to the emerging water crisis, Sunnylands decided to implement the most advanced water conservation strategy available.

Project Facts

  • Sunnylands Center & Gardens in Rancho Mirage, California, is a $35 million, 15-acre extension of the 200-acre desert estate of U.S. Ambassador Walter Annenberg and his wife Leonore.
  • The LEED Gold-certified project uses approximately 20 percent of its water allocation from the Coachella Valley Water District through 100 percent on-site stormwater retention.
  • Five years before the City of Rancho Mirage created requirements for the use of reclaimed water for conservation, Sunnylands was already meeting those specifications.
  • In 2012, Sunnylands was certified as a Groundwater Guardian Green Site by the Groundwater Foundation in recognition of its commitment to restoring groundwater.
