Quarry Garden

Shanghai, China
Close Me!

Once vital to China’s industrial economy, quarries are now frequently abandoned. Depleted of their mineral resources, many of these quarries fill with water and form dangerous lakes, while others become landfills that subject nearby residents to pollution.

Photo: China Witty

Close Me!

In Shanghai, landscape architects at Beijing Tsinghua Urban Planning & Design Institute (THUPDI) and Tsinghua University saw an opportunity with an abandoned quarry that offers scenic views of the Shanghai Chen Mountain Botanical Garden. Its central pool had been fenced off from people for more than twenty years.

Photo: ASLA 2012 Professional General Design Honor Award. Quarry Garden / Yao Chen

Their project transformed the 10.5-acre quarry into an ecological public garden that is sensitive to its historical and cultural context.

Project Facts

  • The 10-acre Quarry Garden is located at the center of Shanghai Chen Mountain Botanical Garden.
  • An abandoned rock quarry was transformed into an ecological public garden.
  • A rocky area near the entrance was transformed into a new “biocoenosis” – a self-regulating ecological community. The land was reshaped and plants were established around its edges.
  • Exposed hills and rock walls were reconfigured as pathways to allow access to the deep pool, the central feature of the landscape.
  • The landscape design pays homage to the Chinese landscape architectural traditions while also putting a spotlight on the destructive history of mining in China.
  • Opened in 2011, 3 million visited the garden in the first year. Ever since, the garden receives about 10,000 visitors every day.
